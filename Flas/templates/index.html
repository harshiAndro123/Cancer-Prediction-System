What is cancer?

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells.

These abnormal cells can invade and destroy healthy tissues.

There are many different types of cancer, each affecting a different part of the body.

Risk Factors for Cancer:

Age: Cancer risk increases with age.

Genetics: Some people inherit a higher risk of developing certain cancers from their family members.

Lifestyle choices: Smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, unhealthy diet, and lack of physical activity can increase cancer risk.

Environmental factors: Exposure to radiation, sunlight, and certain chemicals can contribute to cancer development.

Early Detection is Key:

Early detection of cancer significantly improves the chances of successful treatment.

Be familiar with your body and report any unusual changes to a doctor.

Participate in recommended cancer screening tests for your age and risk factors.

hese tests may include mammograms for breast cancer, colonoscopies for colon cancer, and Pap smears for cervical cancer.

Types of cancer

There are more than 200 different types of cancer. Few most common types are as follows:

01

Breast Cancer

This is the most common cancer among women. It starts in the breast tissue and can spread to other parts of the body.

02

Lung Cancer

This is the leading cause of cancer death for both men and women. It starts in the lungs and can spread to other parts of the body.

03

Colon Cancer

This is the third most common cancer in the United States. It starts in the colon or rectum and can spread to other parts of the body.

04

Prostate cancer

This is the most common cancer among men. It starts in the prostate gland and can spread to other parts of the body.

05

Melanoma

This is the most serious type of skin cancer. It starts in the melanocytes, which are the cells that give skin its color.

06

Leukemia

This is a cancer of the blood and bone marrow. It starts in the white blood cells and can spread to other parts of the body.

Attributes

This project aims to develop a machine learning model to predict the likelihood of cancer in a patient population. The model will utilize a dataset containing the following attributes:

Age

This represents the patient's age in years. Age is a well-established risk factor for many cancers.

Gender

This indicates the patient's gender (0: Male, 1: Female). Gender can influence the risk of certain cancers.

BMI

This represents the patient's Body Mass Index (BMI), calculated based on weight and height. Obesity is a risk factor for several cancers.

Smoking

This indicates whether the patient is a smoker (0: No, 1: Yes). Smoking is a major risk factor for lung cancer and other cancers.

GeneticRisk

This represents the patient's genetic risk level for cancer (0: Low, 1: Medium, 2: High). Inherited genetic mutations can increase cancer risk.

PhysicalActivity

This represents the number of hours per week the patient spends on physical activity. Regular physical activity can reduce cancer risk.

AlcoholIntake

This represents the number of alcohol units consumed per week by the patient. Excessive alcohol consumption is a risk factor for some cancers.

CancerHistory

This indicates whether the patient has a personal history of cancer (0: No, 1: Yes). A prior cancer diagnosis can increase the risk of developing another cancer.

F.A.Q

  • What is cancer?

    Cancer is a disease caused by abnormal cell growth that can spread to other parts of the body. These abnormal cells divide uncontrollably and can form tumors.

  • What are the different types of cancer?

    There are many different types of cancer, named after the part of the body where they originate (e.g., breast cancer, lung cancer, skin cancer).

  • What causes cancer?

    The exact cause of cancer is not always known, but a combination of genetic and environmental factors can contribute to its development.

  • What are the symptoms of cancer?

    Symptoms of cancer can vary depending on the type and location of the cancer. General symptoms may include unexplained weight loss, fatigue, fever, pain, or changes in bowel or bladder habits.

  • Is cancer contagious?

    No, cancer is not contagious. You cannot get cancer from another person through touch or casual contact.
